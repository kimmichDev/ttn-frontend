About Company

TAUNG TAN NI, Tea processing was started in 2004 by producing sundried green tea and farmed since form forefather age. In 2016, Organic tea processing factory was established and registered in 2017 at DICA as a GREEN PINDAYA COMPANY. We are running named TAUNG TAN NI for tea production. Tea producing and distributing name is TAUNG TAN NI. The raw tea-leaf is collected from the small farmers farming in PINDAYA region. Applying Organic method and producing safe food is good for health for the consumers and good supporting for environment. Being Organic, farmers can get better price than ordinary products by doing awareness talks and training as an Agriculture CSR Program of GREEN PINDAYA COMPANY since 2016. During Covid first wave, farmers are facing no market to sell their products because the border gates are closed. In that case, GREEN PINDAYA COMPANY collaborated with the related organizations, doners and Fruits and Vegetables Association started “People donate to Farmers (PINDAYA)” program. We bought the crops from the farmers around PINDAYA area and distributed to YANGON and others towns and overcome their losses. We have been supported until the ninth time. Besides TAUNG TAN NI tea production, we are producing “Sein Ta Lone – Mango”

Vision Mission

Vision - Aim to extend the global market with the production of qualified value added products.
Mission - Extend awareness programs to expand organic tea production and produce local products turn to value added products.
Objective - Aim to increase the household income receiving job opportunity for local peoples’ woman inclusion by producing other local products together with the tea production.

Founder

Woman entrepreneur, NANG THARAPHU TINT, is the founder of TAUNG TAN NI tea production and also Danu ethnic woman. She is the activist for the farmers especially sharing farming technique and the secretary of PINDAYA Tea Cluster and Myanmar Tea Cluster. Her family’s origin is tea farming, in 2004, she started marketing the sundried green tea production and production other potential regional commodities such as ginger, avocado, potato.. till now. In 2005, she could study the systematic tea farming from the UNDP and the others resource persons. After receiving the farming technic, she shared it for her resident’s ethnic tea farmers. At the same time she notice about the obviously decreasing of tea producing (yields) by using traditional old farming method.
